#### The three macro channels

**1. Institutional rebalancing channel (`spy_btc_corr_30`)**
Rolling 30-day BTC-SPY return correlation. As institutional investors (hedge funds,
ETF holders) now hold both equities and crypto, portfolio rebalancing creates
synchronized price movements. When correlation is high (+0.5), BTC trades as a risk
asset and equity signals become informative. When low (~0), BTC is idiosyncratic.
- Bull model importance: **rank #2 of 19** (8.0% of gain)
- The model learned to weight equity signals based on how coupled BTC is to SPY

**2. Liquidity / risk-on channel (`spy_ret_5`)**
5-day cumulative SPY return. Large equity selloffs trigger margin calls that cascade
across asset classes. Crypto, with its 24/7 trading and high leverage, is particularly
vulnerable. After SPY drops >2%, BTC averages −2.7% over 3 days.
- Non-bull model importance: **rank #1 of 19** (7.1% of gain)
- During bear/ranging regimes, equity momentum is the single most important feature

**3. Dollar / financial conditions channel (`vix_level_zscore`)**
VIX level z-scored over 60 days. When VIX is elevated, financial conditions tighten:
lending rates rise, leverage gets unwound, and risk assets face headwinds. BTC-SPY
correlation jumps from +0.06 (calm) to +0.55 (stressed).
- Provides regime context: "are we in a stress regime?"
