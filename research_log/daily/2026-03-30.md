### P-ML13 — Unified Dataset Comparison (F21) ✅

**Problem solved:** The scoreboard had an apples-to-oranges comparison:
- P-ML9 (V2, 16f) on 7-day data: Sharpe +1.583
- P-ML12b (V3, 19f) on business-day data: Sharpe +1.118 vs V2's +0.656

Could not tell if V3's improvement was real or a dataset artefact.

**Approach:** Ran all 4 combinations (V2/V3 x 7-day/biz-day) with matched splits.
Weekend cross-asset features forward-filled from Friday close for the 7-day dataset.

**Result: V2 wins 4/6 comparisons. V2 remains champion.**

| Config | Mode | 7-day Sharpe | Biz-day Sharpe |
|---|---|---|---|
| V2 (16f) | binary | +1.234 | +0.931 |
| V3 (19f) | binary | +0.409 | +0.680 |
| V2 (16f) | scaled | **+1.583** | +0.656 |
| V3 (19f) | scaled | +1.360 | **+1.118** |

**Key insight — why the cross-asset advantage disappeared on 7-day data:**

The institutional/liquidity/dollar channels (F19) are real macro forces, but the
*implementation* at daily frequency has a fatal flaw: BTC trades 7 days/week while
TradFi trades 5. Forward-filling Friday's SPY/VIX values into Saturday/Sunday
creates stale features on ~28% of bars. LightGBM can't distinguish "stale weekend
value" from "genuine low-volatility signal", so it overfits to the forward-fill pattern.

This is **not** a rejection of the macro thesis — it's a data frequency mismatch.
The channels would likely work at:
- **Weekly frequency** (where weekend alignment is a non-issue)
- **Weekday-only trading** (if the strategy only takes positions on business days)
- **Higher frequency** (4h bars where intraday TradFi data is available)

**Champion confirmed:** P-ML9 scaled / V2 / 7-day (Sharpe +1.583, MaxDD −33.6%).
