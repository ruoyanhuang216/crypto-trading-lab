### P-ML14 — Weekday-Only Strategy (F22) ✅

**Idea:** If cross-asset features only work on business days, design a strategy
that trades only Mon-Fri, going flat at Friday close. This mirrors how
institutional crypto desks actually operate.

**Result:**

| Strategy | Sharpe | Return | MaxDD |
|---|---|---|---|
| **V2-24/7 scaled** | **+1.583** | +758.7% | -33.6% |
| V2-weekday scaled | +1.454 | +408.7% | **-25.9%** |
| V3-weekday scaled | +1.036 | +302.3% | -38.0% |
| Buy & Hold | +1.052 | +876.6% | -76.6% |

**Three findings:**

1. **V2-24/7 remains the Sharpe champion.** Weekend exposure captures BTC's positive
   drift (+0.08%/day weekends) that compensates for added volatility. Weekday
   cumulative return is +1396% vs weekend +64% — weekdays dominate but weekends
   still contribute meaningfully.

2. **V2-weekday has the best MaxDD (-25.9%).** Going flat on weekends avoids thin-
   liquidity episodes and weekend flash crashes. This is a 7.7pp improvement over
   V2-24/7 (-33.6%) at a cost of only 0.13 Sharpe. **A genuine alternative for
   risk-constrained institutional investors.**

3. **V3-weekday loses to V2-weekday** (-0.418 Sharpe). Cross-asset features do not
   help even when restricted to weekday-only trading. The P-ML12b result was
   likely a dataset artefact from the smaller bar count.

**BTC weekend return characteristics:**
- Mean: +0.08%/day (positive but small)
- Std: 2.4% (vs weekday 3.9% — lower volatility)
- Sharpe: +0.64 (vs weekday +0.88 — lower but still positive)
- Cumulative: +64% over 6 years (non-trivial)

## Conclusions from the cross-asset research arc (P-ML12a → P-ML14)

### What we learned about BTC-TradFi comovements

1. **Institutional rebalancing channel is real and growing.** BTC-SPY correlation
   increased from ~0 (2019) to +0.45 (2022-24) as institutions entered crypto.
   Portfolio rebalancing by funds holding both assets creates synchronized moves.

2. **Correlations are asymmetric.** BTC-SPY = +0.50 in BTC's worst 10% of days,
   +0.005 in the best 10%. "Correlations go to 1 in crises" is empirically confirmed.
   This is the liquidity cascade: margin calls in equities spill into crypto.

3. **VIX is a regime switch.** BTC-SPY correlation ranges from +0.06 (VIX calm)
   to +0.55 (VIX stressed). High VIX signals tightening financial conditions where
   all risk assets correlate.

4. **Dollar strength hurts BTC.** BTC-UUP correlation = -0.27 (Era 4). USD
   strengthening reflects tightening global liquidity conditions.

5. **The signal is real but not exploitable at daily frequency.** The macro channels
   operate at a lower frequency than daily bar-by-bar prediction. They are valuable
   for understanding *why* BTC moves, but the LightGBM model cannot extract
   additional alpha from them beyond what BTC-only features already capture.

### What this means for the project

The cross-asset research (P-ML12a through P-ML14) produced a **negative result for
feature engineering** but a **positive result for market understanding**:

- We now have a rigorous, data-driven framework for *why* BTC correlates with
  equities (institutional rebalancing, liquidity cascades, financial conditions)
- We know *when* these channels matter (stress periods, institutional eras)
- We know they are *not* useful for daily LightGBM predictions
- We discovered that weekend-flat trading is a genuine risk reduction strategy

**The project now has two production-viable strategies:**
- **V2-24/7 scaled:** Sharpe +1.583, MaxDD -33.6% — for return-maximising allocators
- **V2-weekday scaled:** Sharpe +1.454, MaxDD -25.9% — for risk-constrained institutions
